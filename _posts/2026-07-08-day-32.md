what_i_learned: |
  This morning I spent the majority of my time working on our deep learning code and running multiple experiments for our research project. Because each training run takes a significant amount of time, I used that opportunity to carefully monitor the output, identify errors, and make improvements to the code. One issue I encountered was a KeyError that occurred because my code attempted to access a column named "age_group" after I had accidentally renamed the column earlier in the data preprocessing step. Since the column no longer existed under that name, the program stopped executing before the fairness analysis could begin. To solve the problem, I traced the error back through the preprocessing script, corrected the column name so it matched the rest of the program, and reran the code to verify that every function executed correctly. Although debugging can sometimes be frustrating, each error teaches me more about how the program works and strengthens my confidence in writing and understanding Python code. Throughout the morning, I also used ChatGPT as a learning resource to help me understand error messages, review Python syntax, and explore different approaches for solving coding problems. Instead of simply copying solutions, I compared the suggestions with my own code to understand why the error occurred and why the recommended fix worked. This approach helped me become a more thoughtful programmer because I focused on understanding the logic behind the solution rather than memorizing code. Every debugging session reminds me that programming is built on persistence, careful observation, and continuous learning. As I become more comfortable reading error messages and testing possible solutions, I find myself relying less on trial and error and more on logical problem-solving. By the end of the morning, I had successfully corrected the issue and was able to continue training our model without additional interruptions.

blockers: |
  The primary challenge I faced today was troubleshooting errors while running our machine learning model. Since training the model requires a considerable amount of time, even a small coding mistake meant restarting the entire process after making corrections. This taught me the importance of carefully reviewing code before beginning long training sessions because minor errors can cost a significant amount of time. Although it was frustrating at first, I learned that every error message provides valuable information about where the problem originated and how it can be resolved. Developing patience during the debugging process has become an important skill throughout this research experience. Rather than becoming discouraged, I viewed today's challenges as opportunities to improve both my programming abilities and my understanding of the research pipeline. During the afternoon, I participated in SAIRI Connect, where we watched the science fiction film Mercy. The movie takes place in a future where an artificial intelligence judge presides over criminal trials, and individuals are presumed guilty until they can prove their innocence within a limited amount of time. Watching the film made me think about how artificial intelligence could eventually influence important decisions in society, including areas such as law, healthcare, and public safety. While the technology portrayed in the movie was impressive, it also demonstrated the risks of placing complete trust in AI systems without sufficient human oversight. By the end of the film, it became clear that the AI system was capable of making mistakes and reaching incorrect conclusions, reminding viewers that technology is not perfect. This message closely relates to my own research because it emphasizes why fairness, reliability, transparency, and human accountability must remain priorities when developing artificial intelligence.

reflection: |
  Reflecting on today, I realized that my experiences in the laboratory and the themes presented in Mercy complemented one another surprisingly well. During the morning, I experienced firsthand how even a small programming error could produce incorrect results, reinforcing the idea that AI systems are only as reliable as the data, algorithms, and people who create them. Later, the movie explored what could happen if society depended too heavily on artificial intelligence without questioning its decisions or recognizing its limitations. Although the story was fictional, it raised meaningful ethical questions about fairness, bias, privacy, and accountability in AI. These are many of the same issues my research team is investigating through our work on fairness in deep learning models for ECG classification. Today's experiences reminded me that responsible AI development requires both technical excellence and ethical awareness.I truly enjoyed attending SAIRI Connect this afternoon because it encouraged us to think critically about artificial intelligence beyond programming and research. The discussion following the movie helped me appreciate that AI has tremendous potential to improve people's lives, but it also carries significant responsibilities for the individuals who design, evaluate, and implement these technologies. One of the biggest lessons I took away from the film is that artificial intelligence should support human decision-making rather than completely replace it. Human judgment, compassion, and critical thinking remain essential because AI systems can still produce errors, reflect biases, or make decisions based on incomplete information. As I continue working on my own research project, I want to remember that building accurate models is important, but building fair, transparent, and trustworthy models is even more meaningful. Overall, today was one of the most thought-provoking days of the internship because it connected the technical work I do in the lab with the broader ethical questions surrounding the future of artificial intelligence.
