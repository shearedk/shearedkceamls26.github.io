what_i_learned: |
  Today was our Mid-Symposium presentation, and it was an exciting opportunity to present the progress of our research project. I enjoyed listening to the presentations from the other research teams because each group approached their work differently and shared unique ideas. Watching other students explain their projects gave me new perspectives on how to organize information and communicate technical concepts to an audience. I also learned that an effective presentation is not only about having strong research, but also about presenting it with confidence, clarity, and enthusiasm. Observing the different presentation styles helped me recognize techniques that I would like to incorporate into my own presentations in the future. Overall, today's experience strengthened my understanding of both research communication and professional presentation skills. Presenting my own research also helped me realize how much I have learned throughout this project. Explaining our work to faculty, mentors, and fellow students required me to have a strong understanding of every part of the research process. I became more confident as I progressed through my portion of the presentation and realized that preparation truly makes a difference. Answering questions and discussing our project reinforced my knowledge of our research topic and reminded me why our work is meaningful. The experience also showed me that presenting research is an important skill that improves with practice. By the end of the symposium, I felt proud of the progress I had made as both a researcher and a presenter.
  
blockers: |
  One challenge my group encountered during our presentation involved our demonstration video. Although the video effectively showed our project, it was longer than necessary and moved through some sections too quickly for the audience to fully understand what was happening. Looking back, I believe a shorter and more focused demonstration would have allowed us to explain each step more clearly while keeping the audience engaged. This experience taught us that visual demonstrations should support the presentation rather than take attention away from the speaker. For our final symposium presentation, we plan to shorten the video and better coordinate it with our verbal explanations. Making these improvements will help us deliver a smoother and more effective presentation. I also recognized a few personal areas that I would like to improve before the final symposium. While I was proud that I stood in front of the audience and presented with confidence, I noticed that I occasionally forgot details that I had planned to include in my speech. I also realized that I crossed my arms at times, which made my body language appear less open and engaging. Moving forward, I plan to rehearse my presentation more frequently so that I can remember my key points without relying too heavily on the slides. I also want to become more intentional about using natural hand gestures and maintaining confident posture while speaking. These small improvements will help me become a stronger and more polished public speaker.
