what_i_learned: |
  Today was a very productive day because our team focused on improving both our research paper and our final elevator pitch video. We began by writing a detailed script for our elevator pitch and assigning each team member specific speaking roles so that everyone had an equal contribution. Rather than simply explaining our research, we wanted to tell a story that would immediately capture the audience's attention while highlighting the importance of fairness in artificial intelligence. We developed a creative scenario involving a patient, a physician, and an AI system to demonstrate how a false negative prediction can have serious consequences in healthcare. I enjoyed participating in the brainstorming process because it challenged us to communicate complex research concepts in a way that would be understandable to individuals without a technical background. This experience reinforced that effective scientific communication is just as important as conducting the research itself. Later in the day, we met with the university Writing Center to review our literature review and introduction. They carefully evaluated our manuscript and provided thoughtful feedback that will significantly strengthen our paper before the final symposium. One of the most valuable suggestions was to shorten several of our literature review paragraphs because some sections contained too many ideas in a single paragraph. They also pointed out duplicated references, reminded us to include a structured abstract, and encouraged us to complete the remaining sections of the manuscript, including the Results, Discussion, Limitations, Future Work, and Conclusion. In addition, they recommended adding a workflow figure that clearly illustrates our experimental pipeline from data preprocessing through fairness evaluation, which will help readers better understand our methodology. I appreciated receiving detailed feedback because it reminded me that strong scientific writing requires multiple revisions and careful attention to organization, clarity, and reproducibility.

blockers: |
  Although we made excellent progress today, one challenge was recognizing how much work still remains before our final paper is complete. The Writing Center encouraged us to expand our methodology by including important implementation details such as the number of independent training runs, the CNN architecture, batch size, number of training epochs, regularization techniques, and early stopping criteria. They also emphasized that reproducibility is a fundamental part of scientific research, meaning other researchers should be able to repeat our experiments using the information we provide. Another recommendation was to clearly define every fairness metric used throughout the study, including False Negative Rate and False Positive Rate, while explaining why these measurements were selected instead of relying only on traditional performance metrics. Although this feedback requires additional work, I understand that these revisions will improve both the quality and credibility of our research. I now appreciate that writing a research paper involves much more than reporting results.It also requires providing enough detail for others to understand and reproduce the work. Another challenge involved thinking critically about how we analyze and present our findings. The Writing Center suggested that reporting only the mean and standard deviation may not be sufficient and recommended including additional statistical analyses, confidence intervals, or significance testing to better compare predictive stability and fairness variability. This feedback encouraged me to think beyond simply generating results and to focus on how those results are interpreted and communicated. We also discussed incorporating additional tables, figures, and visualizations to make our findings easier for readers to understand. Fortunately, our team has already begun creating graphs and fairness visualizations that will strengthen both our manuscript and presentation. Although there is still a considerable amount of work to complete, today's meeting gave us a clear roadmap for the revisions we need to make before submitting our final research paper and presenting at the symposium.
