what_i_learned: |
  During this research experience, I learned how important literature reviews are for understanding both the background and the research gap of a study. Through reading previous studies, I learned that deep learning models used for clinical ECG classification can achieve very high accuracy and are becoming increasingly useful for supporting medical diagnoses. However, I also discovered an important gap in the literature. Most studies focus only on average accuracy from a single training run and do not examine whether fairness metrics remain stable across multiple training runs. This means researchers may not know if a model that appears fair in one experiment will remain fair when trained under different random conditions. I also learned that identifying a research gap is essential because it serves as the foundation for developing meaningful research questions and future studies. Another important lesson was learning how scientific papers are structured and what researchers expect in each section. I learned that the abstract should clearly identify the research gap and explain why the study is needed. The introduction should summarize the most recent findings from the literature while highlighting what is still missing in current research. I also learned that the discussion section should compare the study's findings with previous research to determine whether the results support or contradict existing knowledge. The conclusion should summarize the major findings and provide directions for future research. Throughout the process, I learned the importance of maintaining professional, ethical, accurate, clear, and complete scientific writing. These skills help ensure that research is understandable, credible, and useful to other researchers and practitioners. I also learned why data preparation code is one of the most important parts of machine learning research. Before a computer can learn from data, the data must first be cleaned, organized, and formatted correctly. In many ways, the preparation code acts like a translator because it converts raw information into a format that the computer can understand. Without this step, the model would not be able to process the ECG recordings properly. I learned that the code also separates the training, validation, and testing datasets fairly so that researchers can accurately evaluate model performance. This process helps ensure that the final results are trustworthy and not biased by improper data handling.

blockers: |
  The only frustrating challenge I encountered involved working with files in Google Colab. At times, I had difficulty figuring out how to properly unzip the dataset and ensure that all files were loaded into the correct directories. Since the ECG dataset is very large, waiting for files to upload, extract, and process sometimes slowed down my progress. There were also moments when I had to rerun code because files were not located where the notebook expected them to be. Although these issues were not major obstacles, they required additional time and attention to resolve. Despite these frustrations, I gained valuable experience using Google Colab and became more comfortable managing large datasets in a cloud-based environment.

reflection: | 
  Today was a productive and rewarding day working in my research lab. I spent time learning how the ECG data preparation code works and why each step is necessary before machine learning models can be trained. By carefully examining the code, I gained a deeper understanding of how patient information, diagnosis labels, and ECG signals are organized for analysis. I learned that preparing data is much more than simply loading files into a program. Researchers must ensure that the data is clean, standardized, and divided appropriately so that the model can learn effectively. This experience helped me appreciate the amount of work that occurs before the actual model training process begins. I also reflected on how each section of the code contributes to the overall research goal. The code begins by loading patient information and translating medical diagnosis codes into meaningful categories that can be analyzed more easily. It then creates simplified labels for five major heart conditions and separates the data into training, validation, and testing groups. Afterward, the ECG recordings are standardized and packaged with patient metadata such as age and sex. Finally, the code creates batches that allow the computer to process data more efficiently during training. Seeing how all of these components work together helped me understand how machine learning researchers prepare data to build reliable and accurate models for healthcare applications.
