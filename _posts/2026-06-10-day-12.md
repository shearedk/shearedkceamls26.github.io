what_i_learned: |
  Today, I learned about Overleaf, which is an online LaTeX editor that allows users to write, edit, and collaborate on professional documents, especially research papers. Overleaf is commonly used by researchers, professors, and students because it helps create well-formatted academic papers. Before today, I had never used this software, so it was a completely new experience for me. At first, I was unsure about how everything worked because the layout and formatting tools were different from programs like Microsoft Word. However, after exploring the platform and learning about its features, I realized that it can be very useful for organizing research projects. I think Overleaf will help me improve the quality and professionalism of my future academic writing. Another thing I learned today was during Literature Reviews with Dr. Anita Pandey. We discussed different ways artificial intelligence can be used to support research and academic work. One interesting lesson was that using the same prompt across different AI platforms can produce very different results. Some AI tools provide more detailed responses, while others focus on different aspects of the question. This showed me the importance of comparing information from multiple sources instead of relying on only one tool. I found this discussion valuable because it demonstrated both the strengths and limitations of AI in research. Overall, the session helped me better understand how technology can be used responsibly and effectively in the research process.

blockers: |
  Today, I experienced a few challenges while learning new concepts and software. Since Overleaf was completely new to me, it took some time to understand how the system worked and how documents are formatted. Unlike programs that I am already familiar with, there was a learning curve that required patience and practice. I sometimes felt unsure about where to find certain tools and features. However, I reminded myself that learning something new always takes time. Although these challenges slowed me down initially, they also gave me an opportunity to develop new skills.

reflection: |
  One of the most enjoyable parts of the day was working with Dr. Mack after lunch on the ChatGPT assignment. It was interesting to see how much information ChatGPT could generate about goals, careers, interests, and future plans. I was surprised by how detailed some of the responses were and how the system connected different pieces of information together. I think I did a good job completing the activity, especially because ChatGPT did not have access to all of my personal information. When I used the prompt that Dr. Mack provided and requested an image, the system explained that it could not accurately create one without having a picture of me. That experience helped me better understand both the capabilities and limitations of artificial intelligence. I also enjoyed seeing what other people created during the activity. It was fun to learn more about their interests, goals, personalities, and future career aspirations through the designs they made. Seeing the variety of responses showed how different each person's experiences and ambitions are. Another exciting part of the day was being assigned my focus area for the project. I was assigned to the researcher role, which made me happy because research is one of my favorite areas of academic work. I enjoy asking questions, analyzing information, and discovering new knowledge through investigation. Overall, today was both educational and enjoyable, and it helped me gain a deeper appreciation for research, technology, and collaboration.
