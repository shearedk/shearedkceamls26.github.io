what_i_learned: |
  Today was a pretty smooth day because it was my first official day participating in the program in person. One of the highlights of the day was getting to learn more about my fellow cohort members and hearing about their academic interests and career goals. During the Data Analytics Workshop led by Dr. Jamell Dacon, I was introduced to several important concepts that are commonly used in data science and analytics. The workshop focused on the foundations of data analytics using Python, beginning with basic data handling techniques and gradually moving into machine learning applications. We learned how to use NumPy for numerical computing and how pandas can be used to load, clean, and organize datasets efficiently. I enjoyed seeing how these tools work together to help researchers analyze large amounts of information and make data-driven decisions. Another major part of the workshop focused on visualization and machine learning. We learned how to create exploratory data visualizations using Matplotlib and Seaborn, which help transform raw numbers into meaningful graphs and charts. Dr. Dacon also introduced us to baseline machine learning models used for classification and regression tasks. I found it interesting to learn how models can be trained to make predictions and how their performance can be evaluated using different metrics. The hands-on activities helped reinforce the concepts because we were able to apply what we learned immediately. It was also inspiring to connect with Dr. Dacon personally and learn that he graduated from Michigan State University, which is especially meaningful to me because Michigan is my home state. Hearing about his academic journey made the experience feel more relatable and motivating.

blockers: |
  Today was my third day at Morgan State University and my first day participating in SAIRI activities in person. Overall, I did not experience any major blockers or challenges that prevented me from being successful during the day. However, one adjustment I am still making is becoming familiar with the campus layout and learning where everything is located. Since Morgan State is much larger than what I am used to, navigating between different buildings requires some planning. I also spent time learning the daily routine and schedule that comes with being part of an in-person research program. While these adjustments are minor, they still require time and attention as I become more comfortable in my new environment. Another small challenge was adjusting to the walk between my dorm and research lab. The walk is approximately fifteen minutes, which is manageable, but it requires me to be more mindful of my time management. I want to make sure I leave early enough each day so that I arrive on time and prepared for all activities. In addition, I am still learning how different parts of the campus connect with one another. Getting accustomed to the pace of the program and balancing social interactions with academic responsibilities is also part of the adjustment process. Although these are not significant obstacles, they are areas that I will continue improving as the summer progresses. I expect that within the next week, these routines will begin to feel much more natural.

reflection: |
  Today was a very enjoyable and productive day overall. Morgan State University's campus is beautiful, and I appreciated having the opportunity to experience it in person after arriving earlier this week. One of my favorite parts of the day was meeting the members of my research lab and getting introduced to my mentors. Everyone was welcoming, supportive, and willing to answer questions, which made me feel comfortable and excited about the work ahead. Their positive attitudes gave me confidence that this summer will be both academically rewarding and personally meaningful. I can already see that this experience will help me grow as a student, researcher, and future professional. Another memorable moment from the day was participating in the team-building activity where we competed to build the tallest tower using noodles, tape, and marshmallows. It was exciting to work together, communicate ideas, and solve problems as a group. Our team ended up winning first place, which made the activity even more enjoyable. Beyond the competition, the exercise highlighted the importance of teamwork, creativity, and effective communication. I also enjoyed meeting students from other research groups and learning about the projects they will be working on throughout the summer. Overall, today was a great start to the in-person portion of the program, and it left me feeling optimistic about the weeks ahead.
