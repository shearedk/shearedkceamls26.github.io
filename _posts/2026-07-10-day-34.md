what_i_learned: |
  Today began with our weekly research progress meeting, where we reflected on everything we accomplished this week and discussed our priorities for next week. During the meeting, we outlined several important tasks, including creating our final elevator pitch, improving our research presentation, and adding more tables and figures to both our research paper and symposium slides. I also spent time thinking about my own professional growth throughout this internship. One area that I want to continue improving is my public speaking. I have become much more comfortable presenting research, but I still want to learn how to speak at a steady pace, organize my thoughts more clearly, and confidently communicate complex ideas without feeling rushed. I realized that being an effective researcher also means being an effective communicator who can explain technical concepts to audiences with different levels of experience. One experience today especially stood out to me. One of my lab partners had to leave early, so I volunteered to present their portion of our discussion even though I had not prepared for it beforehand. Although I was able to complete the presentation, I recognized that I was not as confident as I wanted to be because I had not practiced that section. That experience reminded me that unexpected situations can happen at any time, and being overly prepared is always better than being underprepared. I want to develop the habit of understanding every section of our project rather than only the parts I am personally assigned. Doing so will make me a stronger teammate and a more confident presenter. I viewed today's experience as an important learning opportunity that will help me continue growing throughout the remainder of the internship.

blockers: |
  One challenge I faced today involved preparing for future presentations. While I have become more confident speaking in front of groups, I sometimes speak too quickly when I become excited or nervous. As a result, I occasionally skip over important details or fail to emphasize the key points that I want my audience to remember. To improve this skill, I plan to spend more time rehearsing my presentations out loud instead of only reading through my slides. I also want to slow my pace, make better eye contact with my audience, and pause naturally between ideas so that my message is easier to understand. I believe these small improvements will make a significant difference in my confidence and overall presentation skills. Another challenge today was strengthening my understanding of the programming code behind our research rather than simply running it successfully. To address this, I used ChatGPT to explain our Python code line by line in simple language. Instead of only learning what each command does, I focused on understanding why each step was necessary and how it contributed to creating our visualizations. Breaking the code into smaller pieces helped me better understand concepts such as plotting, formatting graphs, labeling axes, displaying fairness metrics, and comparing model performance across multiple training runs. This approach made the programming feel much less intimidating and gave me greater confidence in explaining both the code and the results to others. I learned that truly understanding the logic behind the code is just as valuable as writing the code itself because it allows me to communicate my research more effectively.

reflection: |
  Today's activities reminded me that research is much more than collecting data and training machine learning models. It also requires clear communication, teamwork, preparation, and continuous learning. I was especially excited that our team began developing ideas for our final elevator pitch because it challenged us to explain several weeks of research in only 90 seconds. One hook that I wrote and shared with the group was: "When most people hear that an AI model is 95% accurate, they assume it works well for everyone. But what if that same model consistently misses heart conditions in certain groups of patients?" I believe this introduction immediately captures the audience's attention while highlighting the central question of our research on fairness in artificial intelligence. I hope we continue refining this idea because it clearly explains why our work is meaningful beyond simply building an accurate model. Looking back on this week, I am proud of how much I have grown both technically and professionally. At the beginning of the internship, many of the programming concepts and machine learning terminology felt unfamiliar, but I now have a much stronger understanding of how deep learning models are developed, evaluated, and improved. I also feel more comfortable discussing fairness, reproducibility, and bias mitigation in healthcare artificial intelligence. My goal moving forward is to continue strengthening my public speaking skills so I can communicate my research with the same confidence that I have when working in the lab. I want every presentation to demonstrate not only what I have learned but also the passion I have developed for artificial intelligence research. Overall, today reminded me that becoming a successful researcher requires curiosity, preparation, resilience, and a willingness to continue learning every day.
