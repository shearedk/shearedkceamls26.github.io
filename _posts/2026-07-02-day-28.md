what_i_learned: |
  Today I dedicated most of my time to conducting a literature search to strengthen the background and discussion sections of our research project. I primarily used Google Scholar to locate recent peer-reviewed journal articles related to deep learning, ECG classification, fairness in artificial intelligence, and reproducibility in machine learning. To make my search more effective, I used keywords such as "ECG classification deep learning," "AI fairness in healthcare," "fairness metrics machine learning," "PTB-XL dataset," and "deep learning reproducibility." I also filtered my results to prioritize recent publications, highly cited papers, and articles published in reputable journals. After identifying relevant papers, I carefully read the abstracts before deciding whether the articles were applicable to our research question. This organized search strategy helped me efficiently identify high-quality sources that supported both the technical and ethical aspects of our project. In addition to Google Scholar, I reviewed reference lists from key research papers to identify additional articles that were frequently cited within the field. This snowball searching technique introduced me to several foundational studies that I may not have found through keyword searches alone. I organized my articles by topic, including fairness evaluation, reproducibility, ECG classification, and healthcare bias, which made it easier to compare findings across different studies. One article emphasized that high predictive accuracy does not necessarily indicate that an AI model performs fairly across different demographic groups, reinforcing the importance of subgroup analysis in healthcare. Another article explained that fairness metrics can fluctuate across repeated model training runs because of random initialization, highlighting the importance of reproducibility in machine learning research. Reading these papers helped me better understand how my research fits into the broader scientific literature and why our project addresses an important gap.

blockers: |
  A significant portion of today was spent troubleshooting errors in my Python code while running our deep learning model. At first, the code repeatedly failed because of issues involving variable definitions, file paths, and mismatched function parameters. Rather than immediately asking for help, I carefully read each error message and traced the problem back to the specific section of code where the error originated. I compared my code with earlier working versions and reviewed the logic line by line to identify inconsistencies. When necessary, I consulted the project documentation and discussed the issue with my faculty mentor to confirm that my corrections were appropriate. After making several revisions and testing the code multiple times, I was able to resolve the errors and successfully continue training the model. Although debugging can sometimes feel frustrating, today's experience reminded me that programming requires patience and careful problem-solving. Every error message provided useful information that guided me toward the correct solution rather than simply indicating failure. I also learned the importance of testing code incrementally instead of making several changes at once, which makes it easier to identify the exact source of a problem. Once the code was functioning properly again, I documented the corrections so that similar issues could be resolved more quickly in the future. These experiences strengthened both my programming skills and my confidence in working with machine learning models. Looking back, I realized that debugging is an essential part of the research process because reliable code is necessary for producing trustworthy scientific results. 

reflection: |

Conducting a thorough literature review allowed me to better understand the existing knowledge in my field while identifying the specific gap that our project seeks to address. Reading multiple research articles also improved my ability to critically evaluate scientific evidence instead of accepting findings at face value. I found it rewarding to connect ideas from published studies to the work that our research team is currently conducting. Every article I read strengthened my understanding of why fairness and reproducibility are essential considerations when developing artificial intelligence systems for healthcare. Instead of becoming discouraged by repeated errors, I approached each problem as an opportunity to improve my programming and analytical skills. Successfully debugging the code gave me greater confidence in my ability to work independently while also recognizing when collaboration and mentorship are valuable. As we continue preparing for the final symposium, I feel more confident in both the technical and scientific aspects of our project. I look forward to continuing our experiments, analyzing our results, and communicating our findings effectively. Overall, today was a productive day that helped me grow as both a programmer and an undergraduate researcher.
