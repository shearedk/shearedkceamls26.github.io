what_i_learned: |
  Today was another rewarding day because it combined research, professional development, and opportunities to learn from others. During the afternoon, my team continued working on the methodology section of our research paper, making sure that each part of our experimental design was explained clearly and accurately. We focused on describing our data preprocessing pipeline, model training procedures, fairness evaluation metrics, and overall workflow in a way that would allow other researchers to reproduce our work. As we continued writing, I realized how important precision is in scientific writing because even small details can influence how readers interpret a study. Developing the methodology also reminded me that research is not only about obtaining results but also about documenting every step of the process with transparency and accuracy. This experience strengthened my understanding of how careful documentation contributes to the credibility and reliability of scientific research. Another highlight of the day was attending the elevator pitch showcase, where all of the research groups presented their videos. I genuinely enjoyed watching the creativity that each team brought to communicating their research because every presentation had a unique style and approach. Some groups used humor, while others relied on storytelling, demonstrations, or visual effects to explain complex scientific concepts in an engaging way. Watching these presentations reminded me that effective communication is one of the most valuable skills a researcher can develop, regardless of the field they study. I was proud of the work my team produced, but I was equally inspired by the creativity and passion demonstrated by the other fellows. Seeing how everyone translated complicated research into presentations that could be understood by broader audiences motivated me to continue improving my own communication and presentation skills.

blockers: |
  Fortunately, today did not involve any significant obstacles that delayed our progress, but there were still areas that required thoughtful attention. While writing the methodology, we wanted to ensure that our descriptions were detailed enough for reproducibility without becoming unnecessarily repetitive or difficult to follow. Finding the right balance between technical accuracy and readability required several revisions and careful discussions among our team members. We also revisited portions of our previous writing to make sure the methodology aligned with the introduction and literature review, creating a consistent narrative throughout the manuscript. Although these revisions required extra time, they ultimately strengthened the quality of our paper. I appreciated working through these challenges because they reinforced the importance of collaboration and careful editing in scientific research. Another challenge was reflecting on how to effectively communicate technical concepts to audiences with different levels of scientific knowledge. After watching the elevator pitch showcase, I realized that presenting research is not only about explaining methods and results but also about connecting with the audience and helping them understand why the work matters. Every team approached this challenge differently, and it made me think about ways I can continue improving my own public speaking and storytelling skills. Rather than viewing this as a weakness, I see it as an area for continued growth throughout my academic and professional career. Experiences like today's encourage me to become more confident when presenting research and to continue practicing until I can communicate complex ideas with clarity and confidence.

reflection: |
  One of the most memorable parts of today was meeting with Dr. Chris Crawford. Listening to his experiences and career journey gave me valuable insight into how computer programming and technology are integrated into everyday life across many different industries. Before this program, I often associated programming only with software development or computer science, but today's discussion helped me understand that computational thinking has become an essential skill in healthcare, research, business, and many other professions. His presentation reinforced the importance of continuously learning new technical skills because technology is constantly evolving. I also appreciated how he discussed the importance of adapting to change and remaining open to opportunities that may not have been part of an original career plan. His advice encouraged me to continue building skills that will make me a more versatile researcher and future healthcare professional. I especially appreciated Dr. Crawford's discussion about entrepreneurship, career development, and the current job market. His advice reminded me that success is not determined solely by academic achievement but also by developing leadership, communication, problem-solving, and business skills. As someone who hopes to build a career that combines healthcare, research, and eventually entrepreneurship, I found his perspective both encouraging and motivating. It inspired me to think more intentionally about how the knowledge and experiences I am gaining through SAIRI can prepare me for opportunities beyond graduate school. Reflecting on today, I feel grateful for the chance to learn not only through research but also from professionals who have successfully applied their skills in meaningful ways. Overall, today reminded me that becoming a successful professional requires continuous learning, curiosity, and the willingness to embrace opportunities both inside and outside the laboratory.
