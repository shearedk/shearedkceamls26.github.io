what_i_learned: |
  Today was focused on preparing for our final research symposium by refining our presentation and ensuring that every slide clearly communicated our research findings. As a team, we carefully reviewed the presentation from beginning to end, making final edits and improving the flow between speakers. We also met with our graduate mentor, who provided constructive feedback on both the content and delivery of our presentation. Their suggestions encouraged us to simplify some technical explanations, strengthen transitions between slides, and make sure each section clearly connected back to our overall research question. Receiving feedback before the symposium reminded me that even a well-prepared presentation can always be improved through collaboration and revision. By the end of the session, I felt much more confident that our presentation accurately represented the quality and significance of our research. My primary responsibility was presenting the project introduction, the research gap, and the challenges and adaptations section. While reviewing these slides, I gained a deeper appreciation for how important the beginning of a presentation is because it establishes the context for everything that follows. I wanted to make sure the audience understood that our research was not simply about developing another artificial intelligence model, but about investigating whether models that appear consistently accurate are also consistently fair across different patient populations. Explaining the limitations of prior research also helped reinforce my understanding of how scientific knowledge develops over time. Previous studies made important contributions to ECG classification, but many focused primarily on predictive accuracy, relied on a single training run, or did not combine fairness, explainability, robustness, and reproducibility into one evaluation framework. Reviewing these concepts strengthened my understanding of our project's contribution and reminded me that identifying meaningful research gaps is one of the foundations of impactful scientific research.

blockers: |
  One challenge I experienced today was making sure my presentation sounded natural rather than memorized. Although I had written a detailed script, I realized that simply reading from it would make my presentation feel less engaging and reduce my connection with the audience. Instead, I focused on understanding the ideas behind each section so I could explain them conversationally while still remaining scientifically accurate. This required practicing multiple times and making small adjustments to the wording until it felt comfortable and authentic. I also had to work on controlling my pacing because I sometimes speak too quickly when I become nervous. Practicing with my teammates and receiving feedback from our graduate mentor helped me recognize these habits and gave me strategies to improve before the final symposium. Another challenge was making sure my slides aligned perfectly with both our manuscript and the presentations of my teammates. Since each of us was responsible for different sections, it was important that the presentation felt like one cohesive story rather than several separate presentations combined together. We carefully reviewed transitions, terminology, and key findings to ensure consistency throughout the entire presentation. I also wanted to be certain that the statistics I presented, such as the variation in False Negative Rate across repeated training runs, matched exactly what was reported in our research paper. Although reviewing these details required additional time, it increased my confidence that we were presenting accurate and reliable information. These final revisions demonstrated that strong presentations are built through teamwork, careful preparation, and a commitment to accuracy.

reflection: |
  Reflecting on today, I feel both excited and nervous as the final symposium approaches. Looking back at the beginning of this internship, I never imagined that I would be presenting research involving deep learning, fairness evaluation, explainability, robustness testing, and statistical validation. Over the past several weeks, I have learned how to read scientific literature, contribute to a research manuscript, understand machine learning concepts, and communicate technical information to different audiences. Preparing my presentation today reminded me of how much I have grown both academically and personally throughout this experience. While I still experience nervousness before speaking in front of an audience, I now have much greater confidence in my understanding of the research itself. Knowing the material thoroughly gives me reassurance that I can explain our findings clearly and answer questions thoughtfully. I also feel incredibly grateful for the guidance I have received from my faculty mentor, graduate mentor, and teammates throughout this internship. Every revision, practice session, and constructive critique has helped improve both our research and my own professional development. Beyond learning technical skills, this experience has strengthened my communication abilities, critical thinking, collaboration, and confidence as an emerging researcher. As I prepare for the final symposium, I am proud of everything our team has accomplished together and excited to share our work with others. Regardless of the final outcome, I know this experience has prepared me for future opportunities in graduate school, healthcare research, and artificial intelligence. Overall, today reminded me that meaningful research is not only measured by the results that are discovered but also by the ability to communicate those discoveries with clarity, integrity, and confidence.
