what_i_learned: |
  Today was a very productive day because I was able to see how much progress our research project has made from the beginning of SAIRI until now. During the morning, our main focus was filming our elevator pitch video, which required a lot of preparation, teamwork, and communication. We worked through each scene carefully, including filming outside, in the classroom, and in the hallway to make sure our message was clear and engaging. Our research project focuses on predictive stability versus fairness instability in clinical ECG classification, where we investigate whether deep learning models that perform accurately overall also provide consistent results across different patient groups. I believe our team did a great job communicating a complex research topic in a way that people without a technical background could understand. Creating this video helped me realize that strong research is not only about having important findings but also about being able to explain those findings in a meaningful way to different audiences. In addition to filming the elevator pitch, I continued improving our research manuscript by revising the introduction and literature review based on the feedback provided by the writing center. Their suggestions helped me understand that scientific writing requires organization, clarity, and attention to detail. I focused on improving areas such as shortening lengthy paragraphs, removing duplicated references, adding missing sections, and making sure the purpose and significance of our study were clearly explained. I also revised the introduction by strengthening the explanation of why fairness evaluation is necessary in healthcare AI and why accuracy alone does not guarantee that a model works equally well for every patient population. One important change was making the research gap more clear by explaining that many previous studies evaluate fairness as a single outcome instead of examining how fairness changes across repeated training runs. This revision process helped me become more confident in scientific writing and showed me the importance of receiving feedback from others to improve the quality of research.

blockers: |
  Today, I did not experience any major blockers, but I did recognize some challenges that I need to continue improving. One challenge was balancing the technical details of our research with making our elevator pitch understandable and engaging within a short amount of time. Since our research involves deep learning models, ECG classification, fairness metrics, and bias evaluation, it was difficult to decide what information was most important to include in only 90 seconds. We had to carefully choose our wording so that the audience could understand the importance of our research without becoming overwhelmed by scientific terminology. Another challenge was making sure every team member’s role was organized during filming because multiple scenes required coordination, timing, and preparation. Although there were small moments where we had to redo scenes or adjust our approach, working through those challenges improved our teamwork and communication skills. Another area that required attention was making sure our manuscript fully addressed the writing center’s recommendations. The feedback showed us that our paper needed more explanation about our experimental design, fairness metrics, model architecture, and reproducibility methods. I realized that even though I understand many parts of the project, the reader may not understand our process unless we clearly explain every step. This encouraged me to spend additional time reviewing our code and making sure I understood exactly how each section contributes to our analysis. I also identified that we need to continue improving the visual components of our research, including adding workflow diagrams, tables, and figures that make our methods and results easier to interpret. These challenges were valuable because they helped me think more like a researcher who is responsible not only for conducting experiments but also for clearly communicating findings.

reflection: |
  Reflecting on today, I feel proud of the progress I have made throughout this research experience. At the beginning of SAIRI, I was still learning how to navigate deep learning concepts, Python programming, and healthcare AI research, but today I was able to contribute to a research presentation, explain our findings, and help improve a scientific manuscript. Filming the elevator pitch was especially meaningful because it allowed me to see our research from the perspective of someone outside of our research group. I learned that communicating science requires creativity, storytelling, and the ability to connect technical information to real-world problems. Our research question about fairness in ECG classification is not just about computer models; it is about ensuring that future healthcare technologies provide safe and equitable care for all patients. This experience has strengthened my interest in using technology and healthcare together to solve meaningful problems. I also appreciated the opportunity to improve our manuscript because it reminded me that research is an ongoing process that requires constant revision and growth. The writing center’s feedback helped me become more aware of the importance of structure, organization, and making scientific arguments clear for the reader. Moving forward, I want to continue improving my ability to communicate research confidently, whether through presentations, writing, or conversations with people outside of my field. I also want to continue developing my understanding of the code behind our models so that I can explain not only what our results show but why those results occur. Overall, today showed me that becoming a successful researcher requires both technical knowledge and strong communication skills, and I am grateful for the opportunities SAIRI has provided me to develop both.
