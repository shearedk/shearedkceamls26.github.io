what_i_learned: |
  Today our research group traveled to Washington, D.C., to attend the AWS Summit, and it was one of the most inspiring professional experiences I have had this summer. Being surrounded by professionals, educators, researchers, and technology experts gave me a broader understanding of the many career opportunities available in technology and artificial intelligence. I attended several sessions that introduced new concepts related to cloud computing, machine learning, and intelligent software systems. One of the most interesting topics I learned about was the Strands Agents SDK, which allows developers to build specialized AI agents that can perform tasks such as writing code, monitoring cloud resources, conducting web research, and managing AWS services. I also learned about the Model Context Protocol (MCP), which provides AI models with access to external tools and up-to-date information, allowing them to perform more accurate and useful tasks. Overall, the summit showed me how AI systems are becoming more capable by combining large language models with cloud infrastructure and external tools. During one of the hands-on demonstrations, I learned how developers can create multiple AI agents that work together to solve complex problems. For example, one agent could edit Python code, another could search AWS documentation in real time, while another managed cloud resources such as Amazon DynamoDB and Amazon EC2. I found it fascinating that these agents could communicate with AWS services using natural language commands instead of requiring users to manually perform every task. Watching these demonstrations helped me better understand how artificial intelligence can automate software development, system monitoring, and infrastructure management. Although cloud computing is not my primary area of study, I appreciated learning how these technologies are used in real-world environments. Today's experience reminded me that interdisciplinary knowledge is valuable because AI continues to impact healthcare, research, business, and many other fields.

blockers: |
  Fortunately, I did not experience any major blockers during today's activities because the focus was on learning from presentations, demonstrations, and networking opportunities. However, there were several advanced technical concepts that were completely new to me, particularly those involving cloud infrastructure, AWS services, and multi-agent AI systems. At times, the presenters moved quickly through technical demonstrations, making it difficult to fully understand every detail during the sessions. Even though I was not familiar with all of the terminology, I concentrated on understanding the overall concepts rather than every technical implementation. This experience reminded me that it is normal to encounter unfamiliar topics when exploring new fields. I plan to continue reviewing these concepts independently so I can strengthen my understanding of cloud computing and AI technologies. Another minor challenge was realizing just how broad the technology field has become. There were many different workshops and presentations happening simultaneously, and I wished I had enough time to attend all of them. Each session introduced new tools and innovations that demonstrated how rapidly artificial intelligence and cloud technologies continue to evolve. Rather than feeling overwhelmed, I viewed this as motivation to continue learning beyond the classroom. I also recognized that no one is expected to know everything, especially in a field that changes so quickly. This mindset helped me remain curious and open to learning throughout the day.

reflection: |
  Reflecting on today, I feel extremely grateful for the opportunity to attend the AWS Summit in Washington, D.C. It was inspiring to see professionals from different backgrounds come together to share ideas, innovations, and real-world applications of technology. The experience expanded my perspective on how artificial intelligence can be integrated into industries beyond healthcare, including cloud computing, cybersecurity, and software engineering. I would like to sincerely thank Dr. Mack for making this opportunity possible and for encouraging us to participate in experiences outside of the classroom. Attending the summit reminded me that continuous learning and professional development are essential for future success. I left feeling inspired to continue expanding my technical knowledge while connecting it to my own research interests. I also reflected on how much I have grown throughout this summer research experience. Before participating in this program, many of the technologies discussed at the summit would have seemed intimidating or completely unfamiliar. Now, I find myself asking thoughtful questions, making connections between different fields, and becoming more confident when learning new technical concepts. Experiences like today's motivate me to remain curious and continue building skills that will benefit my future career. I am proud of the progress I have made as a researcher, and I look forward to applying what I learned as I continue developing my project. Overall, today was both educational and motivating, and it reinforced my excitement about pursuing research and lifelong learning.
