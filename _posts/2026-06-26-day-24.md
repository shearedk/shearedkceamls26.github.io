what_i_learned: |
  Today my team finalized our research presentation for the upcoming Mid-Symposium presentation. We carefully reviewed every slide, refined our explanations, and made sure our presentation clearly communicated the purpose, methods, findings, and significance of our research. Throughout this process, I learned that preparing an effective research presentation requires much more than creating slides. It involves organizing information in a logical way, explaining technical concepts clearly, and ensuring that every team member understands their role. I also gained a deeper understanding of my own research because explaining it to others forced me to think critically about why each part of the project is important. By the end of the day, I felt much more confident in both the content of our presentation and my ability to discuss our work. We also practiced presenting in front of two other research groups, which was both challenging and valuable. Speaking in front of other students made me nervous at first because I wanted to present my section accurately and professionally. However, the practice session helped me become more comfortable with public speaking and allowed me to identify areas where I could improve my delivery. Receiving feedback before the actual symposium gave our team the opportunity to make final adjustments and strengthen our presentation. One of the highlights of the day was speaking with Dr. Kofi Nyarko, who provided encouraging words and thoughtful insights about our research. His advice reminded me that research is a continuous learning process, and that constructive feedback is essential for growth as a researcher.

blockers: |
  Although we completed the presentation, I faced a few personal challenges throughout the day. My biggest obstacle was managing my nervousness while presenting in front of other research groups. I occasionally worried about forgetting important details or not explaining complex concepts clearly enough for the audience to understand. These concerns made me realize that confidence comes from repeated practice and a strong understanding of the material. Fortunately, practicing with my team helped reduce some of those fears and allowed me to become more comfortable speaking about the project. By the end of the practice session, I felt more prepared than I did at the beginning of the day. Another challenge was making sure our presentation met the high standards we had set for ourselves. As a team, we spent time revising our slides, improving transitions, and making sure our explanations were concise while still being scientifically accurate. Although these revisions required extra effort, they ultimately strengthened the overall quality of our presentation. I truly appreciated my teammates because they encouraged one another to continue improving rather than settling for something that was simply acceptable. Their dedication motivated me to put forth my best effort as well. Looking back, I believe these final revisions will make us stronger presenters during the symposium.

reflection: |
  Reflecting on today, I feel proud of how much progress our team has made since the beginning of the project. Preparing for the Mid-Symposium reminded me that research is not only about conducting experiments but also about communicating findings effectively to others. I am grateful for the opportunity to practice before the official presentation because it gave me valuable experience speaking in front of an academic audience. I especially appreciated meeting with Dr. Kofi Nyarko, whose encouragement and thoughtful feedback increased my confidence and motivated me to continue developing as a researcher. Moments like these remind me why I enjoy participating in research and why I want to continue pursuing opportunities in this field. Overall, today was both challenging and rewarding. I also left today's meeting feeling thankful for my research team and the supportive environment we have built together. Everyone was committed to helping one another improve, and that teamwork made the revision process much more productive. Seeing how much our presentation has improved gives me confidence that we are ready for Monday's Mid-Symposium presentation. While I still have some nerves, they are now mixed with excitement because I know how much work we have invested in this project. I look forward to sharing our research with faculty, mentors, and fellow students while continuing to grow as both a presenter and a researcher. My goal for the symposium is to communicate our work clearly, answer questions with confidence, and represent my team to the best of my ability.
