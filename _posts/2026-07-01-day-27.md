what_i_learned: |
  Today was a productive day because our team focused on improving both our research project and our understanding of the work we have completed so far. We carefully reviewed our code with guidance from one of our faculty mentors, who helped explain what each section of the model was doing and why it was important. Instead of simply running the code, I gained a much deeper understanding of the reasoning behind the model architecture and the decisions that were made during development. Our mentor also explained the history and significance of our research topic, which helped me appreciate how our work contributes to the broader field of artificial intelligence in healthcare. Hearing the background of previous studies allowed me to better understand the research gap that our project is addressing. By the end of our discussion, I felt much more confident in my ability to explain not only what our model does but also why our research is meaningful. Another important topic we discussed was the training process for our model, including why we plan to train it for 40 epochs. I learned that increasing the number of training epochs gives the model additional opportunities to learn meaningful patterns from the ECG data while still monitoring for overfitting. Understanding the purpose behind this decision helped me connect the technical aspects of machine learning with the overall goals of our research. We also reviewed our literature review to ensure that every citation accurately supported our claims and that the writing flowed logically from one idea to the next. This revision process reminded me that strong research depends not only on good experiments but also on clear and accurate scientific writing. By the end of today's meeting, I had a much stronger understanding of both the computational and written components of our project.
  
blockers: |
  One challenge we encountered today was identifying the best improvements to make to our research before the final symposium. As we reviewed our code and presentation materials, we realized that there were several small details that could still be refined. Determining which changes would have the greatest impact required careful discussion among our team and guidance from our faculty mentor. We also had to make sure that every revision remained scientifically accurate while improving the overall quality of the project. Although these decisions took time, they ultimately strengthened both our research and our understanding of the work. Working through these challenges taught me that high-quality research requires continuous evaluation and refinement. Another challenge involved planning how to improve our demonstration video for the final symposium. During our brainstorming session, we agreed that the original video contained useful information but was longer than necessary for an effective presentation. We discussed different ways to shorten the demonstration while still highlighting the most important aspects of our research and model. Finding the right balance between providing enough detail and maintaining audience engagement required thoughtful planning. We also began discussing the remaining tasks that must be completed before the research project concludes, allowing us to establish clear goals for the final weeks of the program. Having these conversations helped our team become more organized and prepared for the work ahead.
