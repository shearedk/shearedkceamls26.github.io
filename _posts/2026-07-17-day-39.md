what_i_learned: |
  Today our primary focus was completing the methodology section of our research paper, which is one of the most important parts of any scientific study. We carefully documented every stage of our research process, beginning with the PTB-XL dataset, data preprocessing, model architectures, training procedures, fairness evaluation, bias mitigation, explainability techniques, robustness testing, and statistical validation. Writing this section helped me appreciate how much work has gone into our project over the past several weeks because every experiment and decision now had to be explained in a clear and reproducible manner. I learned that a strong methodology does much more than describe what researchers did; it explains why each decision was made and provides enough detail for another researcher to reproduce the study independently. As we reviewed each subsection, I noticed how every component of the methodology connects directly to our research question regarding predictive stability and fairness instability in deep learning models for ECG classification. Seeing the entire workflow organized from beginning to end helped me better understand the complete research process rather than viewing each experiment as an isolated task. One area that I found especially interesting was writing about our fairness evaluation and statistical validation procedures. Instead of evaluating only predictive accuracy, we explained why False Negative Rate (FNR) was selected as our primary fairness metric because missed cardiac diagnoses can have serious clinical consequences. I also gained a better understanding of why we trained the hybrid CNN-Transformer model multiple times using different random seeds instead of relying on a single experiment. By calculating the mean, standard deviation, and confidence intervals across multiple training runs, we were able to evaluate whether our findings were consistent and reproducible rather than dependent on one random initialization. Learning about Grad-CAM explainability and robustness testing also helped me understand that trustworthy artificial intelligence extends beyond prediction accuracy. Overall, today's work strengthened my understanding of how reproducibility, transparency, fairness, and explainability all contribute to responsible AI research in healthcare.

blockers: |
  One challenge I encountered today was translating highly technical machine learning procedures into clear scientific writing. Although I understood many of the concepts individually, it was sometimes difficult to explain them in language that was both technically accurate and easy for readers to follow. For example, describing the CNN architecture, Transformer encoder, fairness mitigation strategy, and statistical validation required careful wording so that the methodology remained detailed without becoming confusing. Our team spent time reviewing each paragraph together to ensure that the writing flowed logically from one subsection to the next. This experience reminded me that scientific writing is just as much about communication as it is about technical knowledge. By working through these revisions, I became more confident in explaining complex research methods in a clear and organized way. Another challenge involved making sure every methodological decision was supported by relevant scientific literature. We carefully reviewed our citations to verify that each preprocessing technique, model architecture, fairness metric, explainability method, and statistical analysis was supported by previous research. This required returning to several journal articles to confirm that our descriptions accurately reflected established methods and terminology. Although locating and verifying these references took additional time, it improved both the credibility and reproducibility of our manuscript. I also realized that writing a high-quality research paper requires patience because every statement must be supported by evidence rather than assumption. Looking back, I believe the extra time we spent reviewing our sources made the methodology section significantly stronger.
