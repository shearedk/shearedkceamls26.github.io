what_i_learned: |
  Today was a very productive day because I learned several skills that will be important throughout the remainder of this research program. One of the first things we learned was how to properly complete and submit our timesheets. Although this may seem like a simple administrative task, I realized how important it is because it ensures that participants are paid accurately and on time. We also completed an Overleaf assignment that helped me become more comfortable using the platform. During the assignment, I learned how to create tables, insert images, use bold formatting, and combine different types of text within a document. Since Overleaf will be used extensively for research writing and documentation, I found this activity extremely valuable for developing practical skills that I will continue using throughout the program. Another major learning experience today came from reading a scholarly article focused on intersectional fairness in machine learning. The article explained how a machine learning model can appear fair when evaluating demographic groups separately while still producing unfair outcomes for individuals who belong to multiple demographic groups at the same time. One example discussed how Black women experienced greater disparities than those observed when examining race or gender independently. I learned that intersectional groups often face amplified bias and that these disparities may go unnoticed when fairness evaluations focus on only one demographic characteristic. The article also discussed challenges such as data sparsity, which occurs when smaller demographic groups have fewer observations available for analysis. This reading helped me better understand why our research project examines fairness across combined age and sex groups rather than evaluating each demographic variable separately.
  
blockers: |
  Fortunately, I did not encounter any major blockers today. The instructions for completing the timesheet and Overleaf assignment were clear, which allowed me to successfully complete each task without significant difficulty. While I am still learning the features available in Overleaf, today's hands-on practice helped increase my confidence in using the platform. Creating tables and inserting images required some attention to detail, but the assignment provided a useful opportunity to strengthen these skills. I found myself becoming more comfortable with the formatting process as I worked through each step. Overall, the technical tasks were manageable and served as a positive learning experience. Although there were no major obstacles, some of the concepts discussed in the scholarly article required careful reading and reflection. Intersectional fairness is a complex topic because it involves understanding how multiple demographic characteristics can interact to create unique patterns of bias. At first, it was challenging to fully grasp why a model could appear fair overall while still treating certain intersectional groups unfairly. However, after reviewing the examples and connecting them to our ECG classification research, the concepts became much clearer. I spent additional time thinking about how fairness instability may emerge when subgroup sizes become smaller. While this required extra effort, it ultimately helped strengthen my understanding of the research questions we are investigating.
