what_i_learned: |
  Today was a very informative day because I learned more about both research documentation and data analysis. One of the most valuable skills I learned was how to import references into Overleaf using a BibTeX file. Before today, I was unfamiliar with the process of managing citations in Overleaf, but I now understand how important it is for academic writing. We learned that references can be imported by creating a .bib file, adding citation information from Google Scholar, and then citing sources within the document using citation commands. This process allows references to be automatically formatted and organized throughout a research paper. Learning this skill was especially important because proper citation management will save time and improve the quality of my future research writing. I also attended Dr. Dacon's Introduction to Data Analysis workshop, which introduced several foundational concepts used in data science and machine learning. The workshop demonstrated how Python libraries such as NumPy and pandas can be used to organize, clean, and analyze data. I learned how researchers inspect datasets, identify missing values, create new variables, and prepare data before building machine learning models. The workshop also covered data visualization techniques using graphs and charts to better understand patterns within datasets. Additionally, I learned how classification models can be trained and evaluated using metrics such as accuracy and F1 score. Seeing the complete process from data preparation to model evaluation helped me better understand how machine learning projects are conducted from beginning to end.
  
blockers: |
  Fortunately, I did not experience any major blockers today. The instructions for importing references into Overleaf were clear, and I was able to follow the steps successfully. Since this was one of my first experiences working with citation management software, I initially needed extra time to understand how BibTeX files connect to citations within a document. However, after practicing the process and seeing how the references automatically populate in the bibliography section, the workflow became much easier to understand. I found the exercise helpful because it transformed what previously seemed like a complicated task into a manageable process. Overall, the activity increased my confidence in using Overleaf for academic writing and research projects. While the data analysis workshop was very informative, some of the programming concepts were new to me because my academic background is in Psychological Sciences rather than Computer Science. Terms such as data preprocessing, feature engineering, and machine learning pipelines required me to pay close attention throughout the presentation. At times, I had to connect the concepts back to research methods and statistics that I already understood in order to fully grasp their purpose. Despite this learning curve, the examples presented during the workshop made the material easier to follow. Watching the process of cleaning data, visualizing results, and evaluating machine learning models helped me understand how each step contributes to producing reliable results. Although there is still much for me to learn, I felt comfortable following the overall workflow and understanding the major concepts being discussed.

reflection: |

Learning how to properly manage references in Overleaf may seem like a small task, but I recognize that it is a fundamental skill for conducting and presenting scholarly research. As I continue reading journal articles and writing literature reviews, having an organized citation system will be extremely beneficial. I also appreciate that today's lesson provided practical skills that I can immediately apply to my current research project. Understanding these processes helps me better appreciate how researchers generate evidence and make data-driven decisions. As someone who hopes to pursue a career in healthcare, I find it exciting to learn how data analysis and machine learning can be used to improve patient outcomes and support clinical decision-making. Overall, today was a productive day that strengthened both my research skills and my confidence in learning new technical concepts outside of my traditional academic background.
