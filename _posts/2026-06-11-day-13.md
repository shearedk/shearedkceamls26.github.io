what_i_learned: |
  Today in the lab, we focused on training a 1D-CNN model using the PTB-XL ECG dataset. A 1D-CNN, or One-Dimensional Convolutional Neural Network, is a type of deep learning model that is designed to recognize patterns in data that occur in a sequence, such as heart signals recorded over time. Instead of looking at images like a traditional CNN, a 1D-CNN analyzes numerical data points in order and learns important features automatically. In our project, the model is being trained to identify patterns in electrocardiogram (ECG) recordings that may be related to different heart conditions. This approach allows the computer to learn from large amounts of medical data much faster than a human could manually analyze every signal. Learning about how the model processes data helped me better understand how artificial intelligence can be applied in healthcare and medical research. Another important concept I learned today was the use of seeds and epochs during the training process. A seed is a starting value that controls the randomness used by the computer when training a model. For example, if two people train the same model with different seeds, they may get slightly different results because the model starts learning from different random conditions. Running multiple seeds helps researchers determine whether a model performs consistently or if its results change significantly between training runs. We trained the model using three different seeds and sixty epochs for each seed. An epoch represents one complete pass through the entire training dataset, meaning the model has had one opportunity to learn from every example in the dataset. By training for sixty epochs, the model repeatedly reviewed the data, allowing it to gradually improve its predictions and better recognize meaningful patterns in the ECG signals.
